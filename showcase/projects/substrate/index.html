Crack Growth Algorithm

Substrate implements a biologically-inspired crack propagation system based on the work of Jared Tarbell and natural pattern formation.

Core Mechanics

  • Seed Initialization: Place initial crack points across the surface
  • Growth Vectors: Each crack has direction and velocity
  • Collision Detection: Cracks stop when hitting boundaries or other cracks
  • Branching Rules: Probabilistic spawning of new crack directions
  • Environmental Response: Growth responds to surface conditions

Biological Inspiration

The algorithm draws from several natural processes:

  • Bacterial colony growth and nutrient competition
  • Stress fractures in materials under tension
  • Erosion patterns in geological formations
  • Root system development in plant growth

Natural Pattern Formation

Physics of Crack Formation

Crack formation is governed by fundamental physical principles:

  • Stress Concentration: Cracks form at points of highest stress
  • Energy Minimization: Crack paths follow routes of least resistance
  • Fracture Mechanics: Growth depends on material properties
  • Scale Invariance: Similar patterns appear at all scales

These principles create the characteristic branching and avoiding behaviors seen in natural crack systems.

Biological Growth Patterns

Living systems exhibit similar growth patterns:

  • Bacterial Colonies: Nutrient-seeking growth with competition
  • Neural Networks: Axon pathfinding in brain development
  • Root Systems: Water and nutrient acquisition strategies
  • Fungal Networks: Mycelial exploration and resource sharing
  • Blood Vessels: Branching to maximize circulation efficiency

Applications & Insights

Understanding crack patterns has practical applications:

  • Materials Science: Predicting failure modes in structures
  • Architecture: Designing crack-resistant materials
  • Geology: Understanding rock formation and erosion
  • Art & Design: Creating organic textures and patterns
  • Computer Graphics: Procedural generation of natural surfaces
